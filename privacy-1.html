Privacy Notice

The Deep Map website stores photos and files that anyone can contribute, and dispays them on a map. The map and the contributed materials are published on the internet.

To contribute items, you must first sign in using your Google, Microsoft, or Facebook account. We don't have access to any details of your accounts with those firms, other than your name and email address.

You are responsible for ensuring that it's OK to publish any material you submit.

Contact

This website is run by Span Arts Ltd., a registered charity in Narberth, Pembrokeshire.

Data we process

If you sign in to our site, we store your name and email address. Your name will be displayed on items that you contribute.

If you submit photos, text, and other files, we will display them on the website.

The data is stored in the UK.

Basis for keeping information submitted by you

We assume that if you've submitted information to this website, you've consented to us:

  • Storing the information you submit
  • Displaying it to anyone who visits this website

You can edit or delete material

To edit the text of an item you contributed, tap or click the point on the map.

To delete a file or photo in the item, [PC] right-click or [Mac, tablet or phone] long-press the thumbnail of the file, and choose Delete.

To delete a map point altogether, delete its text and its photos or files.

To delete the references we hold to your name and email, delete all your contributions.

To change or delete material contributed by someone else, contact them or contact us.

Copyright and Data Protection: your obligation

If you submit material to our website, you are responsible for ensuring that:

  • The copyright holder has given permission; or that the material is free of copyright. Note that nearly all photographs are subject to copyright. If the photographer is dead, the copyright holder is generally an heir. See this advice.
  • The privacy of any living person in a photograph is not infringed under the Data Protection Act. Their consent may be required. See this guide.

Cookies

This site uses cookies so that the map opens where you last left it and so that you don't have to sign in every time.

Third parties
