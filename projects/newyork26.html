Exploring New York City in the 1970s (2026)

An immersive, interdisciplinary exploration of 1970s New York City, examining how urban crises sparked groundbreaking creativity, cultural revolution, and artistic innovation.

This interdisciplinary course explores the powerful relationship between place and artistic creativity through a focused study of New York City from the late 1960s to the mid-1980s - a period marked by economic decline, deindustrialization, and suburban “white flight,” yet also by an extraordinary surge of innovation in music, performance, and visual arts. Students will examine how the city’s unique urban conditions fostered an environment of personal and creative liberation, as well as social and cultural transformation, positioning New York as a global center of artistic experimentation. Through a combination of off-campus experiences—including visits to galleries, museums, neighborhoods, performances, and guided tours—and in-class analysis, students will engage directly with the city as a living text. Students will use this map as a tool to document their experience and learning.
